Sketching the Future

Workshop on Advanced Data Sketching Tools

About

Modern data-intensive applications continuously generate massive streams of data, from network monitoring and cloud infrastructure to digital twins and AI systems. Analyzing this data in real time is often impossible using exact methods alone due to memory and latency constraints. Synopses, including sketches, are compact data summaries that enable fast, memory-efficient approximate analytics with provable accuracy guarantees. They are already widely deployed in modern data systems, yet many powerful synopsis techniques remain underutilized, while emerging AI applications present new opportunities and challenges. This interactive workshop is driven by real-world use cases, bringing together researchers and practitioners to identify where current methods fall short and where new synopsis techniques can have the greatest impact.

The workshop brings together researchers in algorithms, databases, distributed systems, and AI/ML with industry practitioners building large-scale data platforms and AI systems. Through interactive discussions centred on practical use cases, participants will identify high-impact research challenges, discuss barriers to industrial adoption, and shape a shared research agenda for the next generation of synopsis techniques.

Our goals are to:

  • Bridge the gap between research and practice.
  • Identify open problems that will shape the field over the coming decade.
  • Develop a shared vision for the future of the data sketching community through interactive discussions and collaborative sessions.
  • Foster lasting collaborations between researchers and practitioners.

The outcomes of the workshop will be summarized in a post-workshop white paper.
